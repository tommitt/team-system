# Marketing per il crunch del 20 luglio — video ad "ninja commercialista"

## Problem / question

How do we push Dott. Comm. hard in a **~2-week seasonal window** (session run on
2026-07-07; the versamenti crunch peaks 20/7) to an audience that is small,
conservative, and hard to reach cold — with essentially **no existing following**?

## The two hard constraints (they shape everything)

1. **Timing.** The product is pegged to the 20/7 versamenti crunch. On 7 July
   that's ~13 days. Rules out anything slow (brand-building, growing followers,
   long funnels). Only reach accountants in acute pain *now*.
2. **Readiness.** As of the brain's state this session, the MCP exposes mostly
   **placeholder** capability (see the scaffold journal entries + `mcp-user-guide.md`).
   Flagged the risk that marketing hard into the one seasonal window before the
   product delivers can burn trust permanently with this audience. **User's call:
   go ahead with the video anyway**, keeping the ad's promise *emotional*
   ("less stress"), not *specific* ("does your liquidazioni IVA"), so it stays
   honest until the product backs it up.

## Audience insight (the crux)

Commercialisti are close to the worst audience for a "polished video from an
account with no followers" play. They are **liability-bearing** (every output
carries their signature), **data-paranoid** (they hold clients' full fiscal
data), and **peer-driven / conservative**. Consequence: **polish does not create
trust here — peer proof does.** A slick ad from an unknown brand asking them to
connect an AI to studio data reads as *more* suspicious, not less.

## Ideas & options (ranked for this window)

- **Referrals / peer proof — #1, and for this audience it's THE channel, not a
  bolt-on.** Get 3–5 real studios using it this crunch, capture the outcome with
  numbers ("saved X hours on the liquidazioni batch"). That artifact *is* the
  marketing. Tap the colleague's personal commercialista network.
- **Social — right instinct, wrong platform.** Not TikTok/IG (follower problem
  real). Commercialisti cluster on **LinkedIn**, big **Facebook groups**,
  **Telegram/WhatsApp** pro groups. Inside communities, content is judged on
  substance, so the follower gap mostly evaporates. Lead with value (a "come
  sopravvivere al 20 luglio" checklist, a free F24/ravvedimento calc).
- **Video ad — reframe, don't lead the *funnel* with it.** A "stressed accountant
  finds Dott. Comm." spot works as top-of-funnel *if distributed*, but funny/viral
  can undercut the seriousness a liability-conscious pro needs. Best first video =
  a real-task demo (proof), with the comedic brand spot as a secondary asset.
- **Category infrastructure — highest leverage, too slow for *this* crunch.**
  Ordini dei Dottori Commercialisti, category associations, gestionale ecosystems.
  Plant now, harvest next cycle (the calendar repeats: bilanci Feb–Jun, dichiarativi
  to 31/10, secondo acconto 30/11).

## Leaning toward (the decision this session)

**Video-ad-first, warm network second** (user's chosen order). Refinement agreed:
use 2–3 real stressed commercialisti as *research* before shooting so the ad rings
true and doesn't read as AI slop — the network is both the authenticity source and
the distribution.

### The "ninja commercialista" concept (chosen creative)

Super-short vertical video, two-shot contrast + CTA:

1. **Scene 1** — primo piano of an exhausted/nerdy commercialista, big text across
   the shoulders: **"Il tuo commercialista in questi giorni"**.
2. **Scene 2** — same man as a calm "ninja" clicking at superhuman speed:
   **"Il commercialista del tuo concorrente"**.
3. **CTA card** — **"Condividi Dott. Comm. con il tuo commercialista"** +
   **dottcomm.dev** (violet brand card, matching the abstract-violet hero).

Production guide (Google Veo/Gemini + Flow, text added in editor because Veo
mangles on-screen Italian) written this session and delivered to the user's
Downloads folder as `dottcomm-video-ad-guide.md` — **lives outside the repo**, not
tracked here. It includes both Veo prompts, CapCut assembly, a styled HTML CTA
card, and A/B line variants (notably: test Scene 2 as *aspiration* — "Il tuo
commercialista con Dott. Comm." — not just competitor envy). Status: user has sent
the prompts to Gemini to generate the clips.

## Open questions

- **Distribution.** Zero followers = ~zero organic reach. The video only works with
  a small paid budget targeted at "Dottore Commercialista" on LinkedIn/Meta **plus**
  community seeding. Budget not yet set.
- **Readiness vs promise.** When a viewer clicks through to dottcomm.dev, what do
  they actually get? Landing + product must not contradict the ad's implied relief.
- **Where the video lands.** A dedicated landing / capture ("provalo per questa
  scadenza") vs. the current free "copy the prompt" homepage.
- **Warm-network activation.** Sequenced after the video per user preference; needs
  the outreach message + the 2–3 research calls.
